## NTS Reports — Name That Shingle

**NTS (Name That Shingle)** — third-party shingle identification lab (pro.ntsid.com, plus.ntsid.com). Founded by John Senac and T.J. Ware (CEO). Lab near Dallas, TX.

**Process:** Contractor ships physical shingle sample → NTS lab analysis → formal report with:
- Product identification (manufacturer, style, series)
- Shingle type and dimensions
- Fading/weathering analysis
- Production status: **Available**, **Temporarily Unavailable**, or **Permanently Discontinued**
- Manufacturer documentation (discontinuation dates)

**Claim Impact:** Provides indisputable third-party evidence that a roof cannot be repaired → triggers full replacement. Without NTS, adjusters can argue a comparable shingle exists.

**NTS PLUS+** — premium tier with enhanced reporting for umpire/appraisal proceedings.

**DumbRoof.ai Integration:**
- AI identifies approximate shingle profile and age from photos
- Cross-reference against NTS database of discontinued products
- Flag as "Potential Discontinuation — NTS Report Recommended"
- Include NTS order link in 5-PDF set

## The C.A.R. Framework for Roofing Claims

Developed by John Senac. The most defensible methodology for repair-vs-replace determination.

### C — Compatibility
Can the new material work with what's already installed? Checks: nailing pattern, exposure, headlap, interlocking design, thermal movement. Incompatible = full replacement.

**Concrete example:** Traditional 3-tab or laminate shingles with 5" exposure are incompatible with ALL current shingles (5-5/8" metric exposure). Different exposure = different coursing lines = different nailing zones = impossible blend. After 10 courses, the lines are 6-1/4" apart. GAF publishes separate nail placement docs for each size (SS-TS-03 vs SS-TS-03a) — proving the patterns are dimensionally incompatible. Cutting shingles to fit voids warranty, invalidates ASTM D7158 wind ratings, and Haag Engineering's 2024 wind testing showed catastrophic failure of trimmed shingles. **Measure exposure on every claim — 5" is the kill shot.** → Full reference: `references/shingle-exposure-repairability.md`

### A — Availability
Is the product still being made?
1. **Currently Available** — repair potentially feasible
2. **Temporarily Unavailable** — repair may be possible with wait time
3. **Permanently Discontinued** — repair impossible → full replacement

NTS Reports provide independent Availability documentation.

### R — Repairability
Even if compatible and available, can the system actually be repaired? Checks: color match (weathering), profile continuity, aesthetic match standards, manufacturer warranty implications.

### Decision Tree
```
Storm Damage → Compatibility? NO → Full Replacement
                    ↓ YES
              Availability? NO → Full Replacement
                    ↓ YES
              Repairability? NO → Full Replacement
                    ↓ YES
              Repair Scope Appropriate
```

**DumbRoof.ai Integration:** Every scan runs C.A.R. automatically — AI identifies shingle type/age/color → cross-references product database → assesses repairability from photos → outputs: Repair Possible / Full Replacement Recommended / NTS Verification Needed.

## Ludowici Tile — Deep Dive

**Ludowici Roof Tile** — oldest surviving American clay tile manufacturer (founded 1888, New Albany, Ohio). Handcrafted natural clay tiles, 100+ year expected lifespan. Gilded Age prestige material.

### Product Lines
- Interlocking tiles, Mission/barrel tiles, Flat shingle tiles, Pan and cover tiles, Shake tiles
- Many historic profiles discontinued, limited matching inventory

### Hail Damage Patterns
- **Cracking** (hairline to full fractures), **Chipping** (corner/edge), **Spalling** (surface delamination), **Puncture** (large hail on thin profiles)
- Damage frequently on **under-surface** — requires hands-on inspection
- Hail resistance varies by product thickness (Class 1-4 ratings)

### Repair-to-Replacement Pathway (Where the Value Lives)

**Triggers for full replacement:**
1. Profile discontinued → no compliant repair possible
2. Color match failure (decades of weathering patina)
3. 20-30%+ tiles need replacement → full roof is more cost-effective
4. Underlayment exposure during repair → code upgrade trigger

### O&L Coverage (The Big Dollar Items)

When a Ludowici roof is opened up:
1. **Plywood overlay** — pre-1970s T&G plank decking doesn't meet current code for solid sheathing → add OSB/plywood ($8K-$25K+)
2. **T&G gap issue** — wood shrinks over decades → gaps exceed 1/8" max (IRC R905.1) → code-required deck remediation
3. **Fastener upgrade** — copper/stainless required (replaces old galvanized/cut nails)
4. **Underlayment upgrade** — 2-layer 30# felt minimum (replaces deteriorated single-layer)

**Field tip:** Photo T&G plank gaps with a ruler. This single finding adds $8K-$25K+ to a claim.

### Historic Properties
- Secretary of Interior's Standards require matching original materials
- Certificate of Appropriateness (COA) may be needed
- Insurance must pay for original-grade Ludowici, not cheaper substitute
- SHPO records as supporting documentation

### Documentation Checklist
- Exact tile profile ID (reference Ludowici catalog, check underside stamps)
- Hail class rating for specific profile
- Damage photos: cracks, chips, spalling, multiple angles
- After tile removal: T&G decking with ruler showing gap widths
- Skip/spaced sheathing sections
- Fastener condition (corrosion, pull-through)
- Historic designation status
- Discontinuation confirmation (Ludowici or NTS)

## Slate Restoration — Deep Dive

### Hard Slate vs. Soft Slate

**Hard Slate** (Vermont, Virginia quarries) — extremely dense, low water absorption, 75-200+ year lifespan, more hail resistant.

**Soft Slate** (Pennsylvania, New York quarries) — higher water absorption, 50-100 year lifespan, more vulnerable to hail (especially aged 40+).

### Damage Patterns
- **Punctures** (large hail on aged/soft slate) — definitive functional damage
- **Fractures/cracks** — hairline to full splits, radial patterns from impact center
- **Chipping/spalling** — edge chips, surface delamination
- **Hidden damage** — cracks not visible from above, structurally compromised

### Wind Damage
- Nail fatigue/pull-through, hook failure, flashing separation, ridge/hip cap displacement

### Inspection Methodology
- Use chicken ladder or foam pads to distribute weight
- Drone + selective hands-on inspection is best approach
- Systematic: ridge → eave, valleys, chimney surrounds, ridge/hip caps, soft metals, gutters

### Countering "Age and Wear" Arguments
- Document soft metal hail evidence (copper dents can't be attributed to age)
- Cite HailTrace storm data for specific date/location
- Compare damaged vs. undamaged areas (show storm trajectory pattern)
- Use tree-canopy-protected areas as undamaged control comparison
- Reference prior inspection records showing pre-storm sound condition

### Repair vs. Replacement
**20-30%+ threshold** → full replacement. Reasons: repair foot traffic damages surrounding tiles, color/weathering match impossible, flashing systems at end-of-life, individual repair cost approaches full replacement.

### O&L (Same as Ludowici)
- Decking upgrade (T&G gaps), fastener upgrade, underlayment upgrade, flashing upgrade to copper/stainless

## Soft Metal Damage & The Chalk Technique

### Why Soft Metals Are Critical Evidence
1. Copper/lead are physically soft — hail leaves permanent, measurable indentations
2. **Age cannot explain dents** — undamaged copper stays smooth regardless of age
3. Dent patterns reveal storm characteristics (size, intensity, direction)
4. Industry standard evidence recognized by adjusters

### Components to Document
**Copper:** valley metal, chimney cap, step/counter flashing, hip/ridge covers, gutters, pipe flashing
**Lead:** valley metal, pipe boots, chimney step/counter flashing
**Other:** aluminum vents, exhaust covers, galvanized flashing, steel HVAC housing

### The Chalk Technique — Step by Step
1. Lay carpenter's chalk **sideways** against metal surface
2. Draw across in one direction with light, even pressure
3. Chalk **skips over dents** — dents appear as unmarked spots against chalked background
4. Photograph immediately: before/after chalk, with size reference (ruler, quarter, golf ball)
5. Wide shots for density, close-ups for individual dents
6. Repeat on all soft metal surfaces

**Hail confirmation standard:** 8+ impacts per 10' × 10' area, in 3 locations.

## The Brittle Test

### Procedure
Bend shingle sample upward ~90°. Observe: flexes (normal) vs. cracks (brittle) vs. shatters (severely degraded).

### DumbRoof.ai Position (High Integrity)
- Document results but **never use as primary evidence**
- Always pair with independent storm data (HailTrace, weather reports)
- Pair with soft metal evidence to confirm storm event
- Strengthens claims with independent corroboration — never manufactures claims

### Field Protocol
- Use sample from damaged section (not undamaged)
- Conduct above 50°F (avoid cold-brittleness false positives)
- Photo before/during/after, document temperature
- Test multiple samples from different roof areas
- **Never bend shingles on a roof you're not documenting as damaged — that's fraud**

## IBHS Research — Hail Damage Science

### Insurance Institute for Business & Home Safety (IBHS)
IBHS is the property insurance industry's own research organization. Their data is particularly powerful in claim arguments because it comes FROM the insurance industry — carriers cannot dismiss it as biased toward contractors.

### IBHS Sub-Severe Hail Study (2025)
**Key findings that destroy carrier "cosmetic damage" arguments:**

1. **The 10x Susceptibility Rule:** Shingles that have sustained prior hail damage (even damage the carrier considers "cosmetic") are **10 times more susceptible** to functional damage from the next hail event. This means a "cosmetic" hit in Year 1 becomes a leak-causing failure in Year 3 when the next storm hits — and insurance carriers will deny THAT claim as "pre-existing."

2. **Decade of Aging in 2 Years:** Repeated sub-severe hail strikes (below the size threshold for obvious damage) combined with normal UV weathering causes **damage equivalent to a decade of natural aging in just 2 years.** The shingles don't need to show obvious craters — the cumulative micro-damage accelerates degradation at the molecular level.

3. **Granule Loss Acceleration:** Each hail impact loosens surrounding granules. Subsequent rain/wind removes those loosened granules. The exposed asphalt oxidizes faster. The next hail impact on weakened asphalt causes even more granule loss. This is a compounding degradation cycle.

4. **No Such Thing as "Cosmetic Only" Hail Damage:** IBHS data demonstrates that ANY measurable hail impact to an asphalt shingle initiates a degradation cascade that reduces remaining service life. Calling this "cosmetic" ignores the documented progressive failure pathway.

### How This Data Wins Claims

**Carrier says:** "The damage is cosmetic — no functional impairment."
**IBHS rebuttal:** "IBHS, the insurance industry's own research institute, has documented that shingles with your 'cosmetic' damage are 10x more susceptible to functional failure from the next hail event. IBHS data further shows that repeated sub-severe impacts — the kind you're calling cosmetic — cause a decade of accelerated aging in just 2 years. This is not cosmetic damage. This is documented, progressive functional degradation."

**Carrier says:** "The hail was only 1 inch — not large enough to damage these shingles."
**IBHS rebuttal:** "IBHS sub-severe hail research demonstrates that even hail below the obvious damage threshold causes cumulative, measurable degradation. Combined with the Koontz/White aging data showing aged shingles fracture at just 1 inch, the carrier's size-based threshold argument is contradicted by the insurance industry's own research."

### IBHS Impact Resistance Ratings Program
- IBHS maintains a Fortified designation program that rates building products for disaster resistance
- Products with IBHS-verified hail resistance ratings have been tested under controlled conditions
- **Key point for claims:** If a product WITHOUT an IBHS-verified rating sustains hail damage, the carrier cannot credibly argue the product should have been more resilient — it was never rated or warranted against the documented hail size

### Application in claim_config.json
When using IBHS data in forensic findings, cite as:
```json
{
  "title": "IBHS Progressive Damage Documentation",
  "content": "Per the Insurance Institute for Business & Home Safety (IBHS) Sub-Severe Hail Research (2025), shingles sustaining hail impact are 10x more susceptible to functional damage from subsequent storms. IBHS data further demonstrates that repeated sub-severe hail exposure combined with normal weathering causes a decade of equivalent aging in just two years. The observed damage at this property initiates this documented degradation cascade."
}
```

## Shingle Identification & Discontinuation

### Key Discontinued Categories
- **3-tab shingles** — most product lines being phased out industry-wide
- **Organic mat shingles** — ALL discontinued (last produced ~2005-2010)
- **T-Lock shingles** — ALL discontinued. **Automatic full replacement** for any T-lock with storm damage
- Specific dimensional/architectural lines — manufacturers discontinue colors/profiles regularly

### T-Lock = Automatic Full Replacement
Interlocking tab pattern, permanently discontinued industry-wide. No matching material exists. Document tab geometry + NTS report.
