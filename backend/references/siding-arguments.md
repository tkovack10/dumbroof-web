# Siding Supplement Arguments — Reusable Across All Claims

**Version:** 3.0 | **Updated:** 2026-02-21
**Code Source:** Residential Code of New York State (RCNYS) 2020

---

## The Corner Rule (RCNYS R703.1 / R703.2) → Forces Full Siding Replacement

**Argument:** When a carrier approves siding replacement on only one elevation, cite RCNYS R703.1 and R703.2 requiring a continuous weather-resistant exterior wall envelope. Tyvek/house wrap must wrap around outside corners per manufacturer installation instructions and code. You cannot terminate the weather barrier at a corner. Therefore, replacing siding on one elevation requires extending the work around corners onto adjacent elevations — opening full siding replacement.

**Code Citations:**
- **RCNYS R703.1** — General: Exterior walls shall provide the building with a weather-resistant exterior wall envelope
- **RCNYS R703.2** — Weather-resistant sheathing paper/house wrap required behind exterior cladding. Must be applied shingle-fashion with minimum 2″ horizontal and 6″ vertical laps.
- **RCNYS R703.4** — Wall flashing required at all exterior wall openings (windows, doors, deck ledgers, penetrations)
- **Tyvek manufacturer installation guide:** wrap must extend minimum 6″ around corners; laps must be shingled

**When to use:** Every claim where carrier approves partial siding (1–2 elevations) but damage exists on other elevations. The code argument eliminates the carrier's ability to cherry-pick elevations.

**Compound with matching argument:** For partial siding claims, also deploy the matching/diminished value argument when existing siding has weathered and new material won't match. See [matching-arguments.md](matching-arguments.md).

## Aluminum Siding (RCNYS R703.3(1))

- Use line item: R&R Siding - .019" metal (aluminum/steel) @ $12.22/SF
- Always include house wrap @ $0.56/SF
- Always include siding labor minimum @ $519.46
- Include sill flashing - moldable tape @ $11.29/LF
- Include R&R wrap wood door/window frame & trim w/ aluminum @ $27.50/LF
- **Code:** RCNYS R703.3(1) — maximum stud spacing 16″ o.c. for metal siding
- **Fasteners (RCNYS R703.3.3):** corrosion-resistant nails/staples, min 1″ penetration into framing

---

## Vinyl Siding (RCNYS R703.11 / ASTM D3679)

- Use line item: R&R Siding - vinyl @ $7.55/SQ
- Same house wrap and labor minimum rules apply
- If carrier refuses full elevation: use "Metal or Vinyl siding - Detach & reset" @ $4.22/SF as fallback, but push for full R&R first
- **Code:** RCNYS R703.11 — vinyl siding shall conform to **ASTM D3679**
- **Installation:** Per manufacturer instructions (which have force of law under RCNYS R905.1 principle — manufacturer instructions = codified requirement)
- **Fastener specs:** corrosion-resistant, min ⅜″ head diameter, 1¼″ penetration into framing, 16″ max spacing
- **Nailing rule:** Leave 1/32″ gap between fastener head and siding for thermal expansion — DO NOT nail tight
- **Supplement note:** If carrier's scope specifies nailing tight or non-standard fastener spacing, cite ASTM D3679 and manufacturer installation requirements

## Fiber Cement Siding (RCNYS R703.10 / ASTM C1186)

- **Code:** RCNYS R703.10 — fiber cement siding shall conform to **ASTM C1186 Type A** (non-asbestos fiber cement)
- **Minimum lap:** 1¼″ for horizontal lap siding
- **Fasteners:** corrosion-resistant (hot-dipped galvanized or stainless), min 1¼″ penetration into framing
- **Caulk joints:** all butt joints, corners, and penetrations must be caulked with manufacturer-specified sealant
- **WRB required:** RCNYS R703.2 weather-resistant barrier behind fiber cement is mandatory — not optional
- **Supplement note:** Fiber cement is heavier than vinyl — carrier cannot substitute vinyl pricing for fiber cement scope. Different material = different labor rate, different fastener requirements, different flashing details.

---

## Cedar Shake/Shingle Siding (RCNYS R703.6)

- **Code:** RCNYS R703.6 — wood shakes and shingles for exterior wall covering
- **Material:** naturally durable wood or preservative-treated per AWPA standards
- **Exposure:** maximum exposure varies by grade and length — follow manufacturer tables
- **Fasteners:** corrosion-resistant (stainless steel preferred for cedar due to tannic acid reaction with galvanized)
- **WRB required:** RCNYS R703.2 applies — weather-resistant barrier behind cedar is mandatory
- **Supplement note:** Cedar shake/shingle siding is a premium material. Carrier cannot substitute vinyl or fiber cement pricing. "Like kind and quality" (policy language) requires cedar-for-cedar replacement.

---

## Wood Siding (RCNYS R703.5)

- **Code:** RCNYS R703.5 — wood siding (clapboard, bevel, board-and-batten, etc.)
- **Material:** naturally durable species or preservative-treated
- **Attachment:** corrosion-resistant fasteners, min 1½″ penetration into framing
- **WRB required:** RCNYS R703.2 applies

---

## Soffit (RCNYS R703.3.1)

- **Code:** RCNYS R703.3.1 — soffit installation requirements
- **Material:** must be of approved materials (aluminum, vinyl, wood, fiber cement)
- **Ventilation:** soffit vents contribute to the attic ventilation requirement (RCNYS R806). Replacement soffits must maintain or exceed existing ventilation area.
- **Supplement note:** When carrier scopes soffit replacement, ensure ventilation NFA (net free area) is maintained. If existing soffit was vented, replacement MUST be vented — carrier cannot approve solid soffit as replacement for vented soffit.
