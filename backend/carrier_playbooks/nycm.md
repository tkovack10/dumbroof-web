## Known Tactics

1. **Complete exclusion of trades** — On claim #1, NYCM approved full roof replacement, gutters, chimney flashing, and aluminum window wraps but completely excluded aluminum siding despite hail damage documented on all four elevations. This suggests a strategy of acknowledging enough damage to appear reasonable while omitting the most expensive trade.

2. **Full roof denial with "wear and tear" defense** — On claim #2, NYCM denied the entire roof, claiming "wear, tear, deterioration, and lack of maintenance" with "no hail damage observed." This is an escalation from claim #1 where they at least acknowledged roof damage. The denial was issued despite 1.75" hail confirmed by HailTrace over 126 minutes — the same storm event as claim #1 where they DID approve the roof.

3. **Understated measurements** — Carrier used 12.00 SQ for roof install vs EagleView-verified 13.20 SQ on claim #1. Drip edge at 161.90 LF vs EagleView 207 LF. Felt at 7.74 SQ — insufficient for 13.20 SQ deck per IRC R905.2.8.2.

4. **Token payment to appear reasonable** — On claim #2, NYCM paid $597.63 ($97.63 net after $500 deductible) for a door awning — acknowledging hail damaged the awning while simultaneously denying hail damaged the roof directly above it. This creates a powerful logical inconsistency argument.

5. **No O&P even when 3+ trades involved** — Carrier scope addressed roofing and gutters without O&P on claim #1. By excluding siding they kept it to 2 trades, avoiding the O&P trigger.

6. **Inconsistent inspection approaches** — Used in-house estimator Jay Ariola on claim #1 (no third party), then outsourced to Hancock Claims Consultants on claim #2. May switch between in-house and third-party depending on what outcome they want.

## Effective Counter-Arguments

1. **Logical inconsistency — awning vs. roof** — NYCM acknowledged hail damage to the metal door awning ($553.36) but denied the roof on the same structure from the same storm. Hailstones strike every exposed horizontal surface — the roof absorbs equal or greater impact than a ground-level awning. This is internally contradictory and the strongest argument on denial claims.

2. **Logical inconsistency — cross-claim** — NYCM approved full roof replacement on 8 Narwood St (claim #1) from the same July 3, 2025 storm in the same geographic area. Denying the roof at 100 Pennsylvania Ave (claim #2) from the identical storm event is inconsistent with their own prior determination.

3. **Quantified data vs. qualitative opinion** — USARM's inspection produced test square data (H-6/W-0 = 6 hail impacts per test square on claim #2). NYCM's denial is based on qualitative assertions with no documented test squares, impact counts, or forensic methodology. Quantified field data supersedes qualitative opinion.

4. **HAAG Engineering standard** — Circular indentation with mat fracture at the impact center is diagnostic of hail per HAAG methodology. "Wear and tear" produces uniform granule loss without focal impact points — fundamentally different damage morphology.

5. **HailTrace weather verification** — 1.75" hail over 126 minutes far exceeds damage thresholds for composition shingles (1.00"). NWS confirmed 1.75" hail reports in the Binghamton area. Hard for carrier to argue "no hail damage" against verified weather data.

6. **IRC R703.1/R703.2 (house wrap continuity)** — When siding replacement is required, house wrap must wrap continuously around outside corners. This forces full replacement, not partial elevation repair. (Applicable on siding claims.)

7. **Measurement discrepancies vs EagleView** — NYCM's own measurements understated multiple components on claim #1. EagleView certified measurements are the industry standard.

8. **Matching requirement (11 NYCRR 216.7)** — Factory-finish aluminum siding cannot be spot-repaired to match. Full replacement is the only compliant option. (Applicable on siding claims.)
