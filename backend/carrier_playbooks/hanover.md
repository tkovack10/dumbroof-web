### 2. ITEL ASP Product-Specific Pricing
Uses **ITEL Asphalt Shingle Pricing** (ASP) to override standard Xactimate line pricing for shingle install. ITEL product-matches to a lower-cost comparable, setting install at $321.73/SQ vs $337.78 NYBI26 standard. This is a separate mechanism from the NYBI8X price list — it's an additional layer of price reduction on top of the lower list.

**Counter:** ITEL ASP is a carrier tool designed to find the cheapest "comparable" product, not the actual installed product. Challenge the product match and demand standard Xactimate pricing.

### 3. Partial Elevation Siding (2 of 4 Elevations)
Scoped only rear (528 SF) and left (499 SF) elevations = **1,027 SF of 2,200 SF total (47%)**. Front and right elevations excluded despite documented chalk-marked hail dents on ALL four elevations.

**Breakdown of carrier's siding scope:**
- Rear elevation: 528 SF with R&R siding .019", D&R shutters (1 EA), house wrap (528 SF)
- Left elevation: 499 SF with R&R siding .019", house wrap (499 SF)
- Front elevation: **$0** (excluded despite "F" chalk marks in inspection photos)
- Right elevation: **$0** (excluded)

**Counter:** House wrap corner rule (RCNYS R703.1/R703.2) — WRB must be continuous around outside corners. Partial elevation siding cannot achieve compliant continuous envelope. PLUS: smoking gun photo of NO WRB behind existing siding.

### 4. No House Wrap / WRB on Full Perimeter
Updated scope added house wrap ONLY for approved elevations (1,027 SF at $0.60/SF = $665.50). The property had **ZERO WRB behind existing siding** — bare fiberboard sheathing (Celotex-type) documented via open panel investigation photo.

**This is the smoking gun.** The carrier partially conceded WRB necessity (by adding it to approved elevations) but the code requires continuity around corners — which means ALL elevations.

**Counter:** Photo p15_01 proves no WRB exists. Carrier conceded WRB is needed by adding it to 2 elevations. RCNYS R703.2 requires it on ALL. The partial concession actually strengthens our argument.

### 5. No Gutters Trade — Deliberately Omitted
Carrier scope includes **$0 for gutters/downspouts** despite:
- HOVER measurement confirming 157 LF gutters + 46 LF downspouts = 203 LF total system
- 1.5-inch hail exceeding 0.75-inch HAAG soft metals damage threshold
- Aluminum gutters exposed to identical storm event that carrier acknowledged damaged aluminum siding

**Strategic impact:** Omitting gutters keeps the carrier at 2 trades (roofing + siding) — avoiding the 3-trade O&P trigger. With gutters added: roofing + siding + gutters = 3 trades = O&P justified.

**Counter:** Carrier acknowledged aluminum siding damage from hail. Aluminum gutters have the same HAAG damage threshold (0.75"). The carrier cannot logically acknowledge one soft metal while denying another exposed to the same storm.

### 6. Code Upgrades Classified as "Ordinance or Law — Paid When Incurred"
Carrier deferred **5 items** as "code upgrades" totaling **$2,613.68 RCV** — all standard storm repair components:

| Item | Qty | Unit | NYBI8X Price | Deferred Amount |
|------|-----|------|-------------|-----------------|
| Ridge vent - shingle-over | 63 LF | LF | $13.89 | $945.08 |
| Step flashing | 39 LF | LF | $14.65 | $617.06 |
| Counterflashing - apron | 40 LF | LF | $15.57 | $672.62 |
| Cap turtle vent & patch deck | 1 EA | EA | $178.20 | $178.20 |
| Cut ridge slot | 1 EA | EA | $200.72 | $200.72 |

**Counter:** Step flashing and counterflashing are required at every roof-to-wall intersection per RCNYS R903.2 — they are NOT code upgrades, they are standard components of any re-roofing scope. Ridge vent replacing an inadequate turtle vent is a code-compliant ventilation correction (RCNYS R806.2), not an elective upgrade. These items are in standard Xactimate roofing scopes.

### 7. Wrong Tear-Off Category (3-Tab Pricing on Laminated Roof)
Carrier priced shingle removal as **"3 tab"** at **$51.93/SQ**. The existing roof is **laminated architectural shingles** confirmed by inspection photos showing dimensional profile. Correct tear-off rate: **$74.94/SQ** (laminated removal).

- Carrier tear-off: 27.92 SQ × $51.93 = $1,450.34
- Correct tear-off: 27.06 SQ × $74.94 = $2,027.88
- **Underpayment on tear-off alone: $577.54 (31%)**

**Counter:** Inspection photos clearly show dimensional profile (multi-layer tab offset) characteristic of laminated/architectural shingles, not flat 3-tab profile. Wrong Xactimate line item.

### 8. No O&P Despite Multi-Trade Scope
Carrier scope applies **$0 O&P**. With only 2 trades in their scope (roofing + siding), they technically have a basis. But with gutters added (3rd trade), O&P becomes justified.

- O&P = Line Item Total × 10% OH + Line Item Total × 10% P
- Our O&P: $67,215.47 × 20% = **$13,443.09**

### 9. Siding Gauge Mismatch (.019" vs .024")
Carrier scoped **.019" aluminum siding** at $12.22/SF. Our NYBI26 reference only has **.024" aluminum** at $12.47/SF. Carrier may argue .024" is an "upgrade" from existing .019" material.

**Counter:** .024" is the standard gauge available from current manufacturers. If .019" gauge is no longer manufactured in matching profile, .024" is the closest "like kind and quality" replacement. The 2% price difference ($0.25/SF) is negligible.
