## Known Tactics

### 1. Outsource Initial Scope to Sedgwick (TPA)
- Church Mutual uses **Sedgwick** as third-party claims administrator for initial inspections and estimates
- Sedgwick adjuster Kyle McAuliffe (Philadelphia office) produced the original 4-line-item scope
- Sedgwick scopes are deliberately minimal — accessories only, no primary roof covering
- **Counter:** Sedgwick is not the carrier and has no incentive to fully scope the claim

### 2. "Under Investigation" Hold on Primary Components
- First payment covers only "undisputed damages" — explicitly holds back on the most expensive component (slate roofing)
- Payment letter states: "At this time, we are in the process of investigating the cause of the damage to the slate tile areas of your roof"
- This buys the carrier months to commission engineering reports that deny causation
- **Counter:** Demand timely investigation per state prompt payment laws

### 3. Commission Engineering Reports to Deny Causation
- After paying accessories, Church Mutual hired **EFI Global** (Steven Marinelli, P.E.) specifically to deny slate damage
- EFI report concluded hail was "less than one inch" despite CoreLogic showing 1.4" at the property
- EFI attributed ALL damage to "age-related deterioration" and "man-made causes"
- **Counter:** EFI's own collateral indicators show hail (5/8" indentations in window cladding, AC coil fin dents, spatter marks). Their 1969 NBS threshold study tested NEW slate — inapplicable to 70-year-old weathered Pennsylvania Black slate

### 4. Retain JS Held to Minimize Scope (Repair Over Replace)
- After EFI's full denial failed to hold, Church Mutual hired **JS Held** (Chad Brandon, RRO) for a second opinion
- JS Held acknowledged hail DID occur but used "exacerbated" language to minimize
- Recommended individual slate repair (883 slates) instead of full replacement
- Dismissed copper gutter/downspout dents as "cosmetic" (not affecting water shedding)
- Attached Joseph Jenkins "What the Hail?" article to support repair-over-replace position
- **Counter:** "Exacerbated" IS causation — the peril that tips functional to non-functional is the covered cause. 883 individual hook repairs across 29 facets is impractical and more expensive than replacement. Jenkins' own article says "soft slate on an old roof impacted by large hail could be fatally damaged" — which describes THIS roof exactly

### 5. Produce Two Parallel Xactimate Estimates (Repair vs. Replacement)
- JS Held estimator Dave Martin produced both:
  - **RE1 (Repair):** $210,542.54 — 883 slates at $99.64/EA + gutters/wraps
  - **FU1 (Full Replacement):** $913,312.87 — full tear-off + slate at $1,922.90/SQ + scaffolding
- Church Mutual paid only the RE1 (repair) scope at $210,542.54
- The FU1 estimate was available but carrier chose the lower number
- **Counter:** Present independent expert report (Cummins) showing 35-45% damage rate exceeds 30% industry repair threshold. Force appraisal if carrier refuses replacement

### 6. Slow-Walk the Claim (2+ Years)
- DOL: April 1, 2023 → Final SOL: October 16, 2025 (2.5 years)
- Each phase introduced months of delay:
  - 3 months: Sedgwick → first payment
  - 5 months: EFI Global investigation
  - 3 months: JS Held investigation + joint inspection
  - 8 months: Supplement negotiations
  - 12 months: Appraisal process
  - 6 months: O&L processing
- **Counter:** State prompt payment law citations; demand written timeline commitments

### 7. Reservation of Rights on O&L
- After appraisal resolved the building claim, Church Mutual issued a formal **Reservation of Rights** letter on O&L coverage
- Cited policy Section G.10.(c)(4)(a): O&L not paid until property is "actually repaired or replaced" and "as soon as reasonably possible, not to exceed two years"
- The 2-year window from the April 2023 DOL expired April 2025 — carrier may argue O&L forfeiture
- Requested building permits, code enforcement documentation, BCO contact info, and cost estimates before paying
- **Counter:** Carrier's own delays prevented timely completion. The 2-year provision allows written extension. Church Mutual acknowledged O&L at $385,257.02 in the October 2025 SOL — this is an admission of liability

### 8. Hold 100% of O&L as "Recoverable Depreciation"
- The $385,257.02 O&L amount is listed with 100% recoverable depreciation = $0 ACV payment
- This forces the insured to front the entire O&L cost and seek reimbursement after completion
- On a $385K scope, this is an enormous cash flow burden for a church
- **Counter:** This is standard for O&L coverage but the carrier cannot use the "paid when incurred" structure to effectively deny coverage

## Effective Counter-Arguments

### 1. CoreLogic vs. EFI Global Hail Size
- CoreLogic (radar-based science): **1.4" at the property**
- EFI Global (subjective opinion): "less than one inch"
- CoreLogic is objective data; EFI's downgrade relies on inconsistent collateral interpretation
- EFI's own photos show hail indicators (5/8" window cladding dents, AC coil dents, spatter marks)

### 2. The "Exacerbated" Admission
- JS Held repeatedly used "exacerbated" — meaning hail WORSENED existing conditions
- Under insurance law, the peril that tips a component from functional to non-functional is the covered cause of loss
- If hail exacerbated aging slate into fracture/puncture, that IS covered storm damage

### 3. The 30% Industry Threshold
- Cummins found 35-45% damage rate across the slate roof
- Industry standard: when damage exceeds 30% of the roof area, full replacement is more economical than repair
- JS Held's 883-slate repair scope ignored the practical reality of accessing, removing, and replacing slates across 29 facets

### 4. Installation Deficiency Supports Replacement
- JS Held documented "numerous subpar installation practices" including overdriven/underdriven nails across "most roofing facets"
- This actually SUPPORTS full replacement — individual hook repairs on a roof with systemic fastening failure is impractical
- Surrounding slates will break during repair access (JS Held's own 30% waste factor confirms this)

### 5. Code Compliance — Solidly Sheathed Deck (IBC 1507.7)
- Tear-off exposed 1-1/2" tongue-and-groove plank decking with significant shrinkage gaps and knot-hole voids
- IBC 1507.7: "Slate shingles shall be fastened to solidly sheathed roofs"
- Existing plank deck does NOT meet "solidly sheathed" requirement
- 5/8" CDX plywood overlay required over entire deck — this is a legitimate O&L item

### 6. The "Other Half" Tie-In Argument
- Carrier approved ~50% of slate roof for replacement
- The 5/8" deck overlay creates a plane change at every intersection (ridges, valleys, parapets, eaves)
- Flashings cannot span a stepped plane — the non-approved half must be torn back and rebuilt at all intersections
- Mandatory tear-back = ~4,733 SF (~44% of the "other half")
- This effectively forces near-complete replacement even though carrier only approved 50%

### 7. Copper Gutter Strap Reuse Argument
- Carrier's scope includes copper half-round gutter replacement with roof-fastened hangers
- Old copper straps cannot be reused: (a) bent/corroded (IBC 908.5), (b) eave flashing must be reconstructed under new roof (IBC 903.2), (c) strap tails go under the slate per CDA details, (d) cold-worked copper loses ductility and cracks when re-bent
- New straps need longer fasteners to bridge the added 5/8" deck overlay

### 8. Borough Code Official Confirmation
- Mike Italia (BCO, Barry Isett) confirmed via email: **2018 IBC** applies
- OneClickCode report confirms ice & water shield required, drip edge required, underlayment required, manufacturer specs have force of law
- These are enforceable code requirements, not optional upgrades
