## Known Tactics
- **Partial scope:** Acknowledged some components (metal vent damage from hail) but denied primary components (full roof replacement)
- **Xactimate-based scopes:** Uses proper Xactimate format with EagleView measurements — scope looks professional but is intentionally incomplete
- **Selective damage acknowledgment:** Will admit hail damage to metal components (easier to see) while denying damage to asphalt shingles on the same structure
- **Siding spot-repair minimization:** Acknowledged siding damage exists but scoped only 25 SF out of 2,332 SF (1%) — admits causation but disputes extent
- **O&P omission on multi-trade claims:** Listed 0% O&P despite 3 trades (roofing, gutters, siding) requiring GC coordination
- **Phantom charges:** Included steep charges (7/12-9/12) and high-roof (2+ stories) charges that contradict their own EagleView data (6/12 max, <=1 story) — inflates scope appearance while still underscoping
- **Dumpster omission:** Scoped full roof tear-off but omitted debris removal entirely
- **Initial scope strips code-required components (PROVEN on 9 Mason):** First scope excluded ALL code-required roofing items (I&W, drip edge, starter, steep/high charges). These were added to the revised scope after USARM supplement cited RCNYS codes. State Farm's initial scopes are intentionally bare — always supplement with full code citations.
- **Single-elevation siding on initial scope (PROVEN on 9 Mason):** First scope limited siding to 1 elevation (761 SF). USARM supplement using house wrap corner rule expanded to all 4 elevations (2,851 SF). State Farm tests how little siding they can pay on initial scope.
- **Persistent O&P refusal:** 0% O&P on all 3 claims, even 6-trade claim #3. State Farm consistently refuses O&P regardless of trade count. This is the hardest argument to win against State Farm.
- **Window wrap cap at 2 EA (PROVEN on 9 Mason):** Final scope included only 2 window wraps despite full 4-elevation siding replacement (2,851 SF). Property has ~15 windows. State Farm appears to cap window wraps regardless of actual count — always supplement for all windows on sided elevations.
- **Fascia token scoping (PROVEN on 9 Mason):** Final scope included only 16 LF of fascia on a property with 341 LF of eave+rake. This is <5% of actual scope — State Farm acknowledges the trade but minimizes it to near-zero. Always cite full EagleView eave+rake measurements.
- **Gutter scope reduction via REVISED lines:** On 9 Mason final scope, carrier REVISED 102 LF of gutters to $0 (reducing from 292 LF to 190 LF). This mid-scope reduction suggests a deliberate strategy to lower the total while maintaining the appearance of a comprehensive scope.
- **Metal roof misidentification:** On 9 Mason, carrier described standing seam metal roof as "corrugated 26 gauge" — different product category. Always verify metal roof type against inspection photos.
- **Template scoping on multi-property policies (73 Theron = 94 Theron):** 73 Theron and 94 Theron (both Umbrella Properties, same policy #98CVM8649, same claim #52-93M4-63G) received IDENTICAL $1,739.69 scopes. Same line items, same amounts, same denial language. State Farm appears to issue a boilerplate scope across multiple properties under a single policy — never adjusted for property-specific measurements. Always compare scopes across properties on the same policy.
- **Sub-deductible scope as de facto denial (PROVEN on 131 Bernice):** Carrier issued a scope of $1,312.05 RCV against a $1,622 deductible = $0 payment. Scope acknowledged hail damage (pipe jack, gutter guard, 12 SF siding) but was deliberately minimized to fall below the deductible. This makes the "scope" effectively a denial while technically appearing to have processed the claim. The pipe jack acknowledgment is the key vulnerability — it proves hail reached the roof.
- **ITEL vinyl siding pricing:** On 131 Bernice, carrier used ITEL Vinyl Siding Pricing (VSP) to price siding at $7.34/SF instead of standard Xactimate rate. Our NYBI26 high-grade vinyl is $7.83/SF.

## Effective Counter-Arguments
1. **Logical inconsistency:** Acknowledged metal vent damage from hail but denied shingle damage on the same roof exposed to the same storm — if hail damaged metal, it necessarily impacted surrounding shingles. Also: approved roof + gutters + window wraps but denied siding on the same structure.
2. **Code compliance triggers:** Full complement of IRC requirements (R905.2.7.1, R905.2.8.2, R905.2.8.5 for roofing; R703.1/R703.2 for siding)
3. **Manufacturer warranty:** Spot repair with mismatched shingles violates manufacturer requirements
4. **HAAG Engineering standard:** Industry-standard forensic assessment criteria confirming functional damage
5. **House wrap corner rule (IRC R703.1/R703.2):** Siding spot repair violates continuous weather barrier requirement — Tyvek must wrap around outside corners, cannot terminate at partial repair boundary. Forces full elevation replacement.
6. **O&P entitlement:** When State Farm scopes 3+ trades but lists 0% O&P, cite industry standard 10%/10% for GC coordination
7. **EagleView contradiction:** Cross-reference carrier's own EagleView against their scope — phantom steep/high-roof charges prove carrier is padding appearance while still underscoping
8. **NY Matching requirement (11 NYCRR 216.7):** Spot repair of 25 SF on 2,332 SF of weathered siding creates impermissible aesthetic mismatch
