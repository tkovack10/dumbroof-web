## Known Tactics

### Material Misidentification
- **Critical pattern:** TheBest Claims Solutions inspector misidentified roofing materials on TWO structures in the same claim.
  - Primary dwelling: Actual laminated comp shingles scoped as "corrugated fiberglass"
  - Detached garage: Actual metal roofing panels scoped as "corrugated fiberglass"
- **Impact:** Wrong material = wrong pricing = dramatically understated RCV
- **Counter:** Side-by-side photos of actual material vs. what carrier scoped. Force re-inspection with contractor present.

### Extreme Area Undercounting
- Garage roof scoped at 96 SF — actual EagleView-verified area is 2,388 SF (25x undercount)
- This suggests the inspector may have measured a small accessory structure or section and attributed it to the garage
- **Counter:** EagleView aerial measurements with structure identification

### Selective Structure Scoping
- Carrier scoped garage gutters but completely omitted dwelling gutters (283 LF)
- Carrier scoped back porch roof but completely omitted patio cover (110 SF)
- Carrier scoped shed roof repair but missed the two primary structures
- **Pattern:** Scope the cheapest, easiest items; omit the expensive trades
- **Counter:** Document ALL structures with photos and match to EagleView structures

### Zero Code-Required Components
- Carrier scope included NO underlayment, NO ice & water barrier, NO drip edge, NO starter strip, NO ridge cap, NO flashing on ANY structure
- This is either an intentionally minimal scope or a fundamental misunderstanding of RCNYS requirements
- **Counter:** Code citation table mapping every omitted component to its RCNYS requirement

### No O&P Despite Multi-Trade Claim
- 5 trades identified, carrier included zero O&P
- **Counter:** Industry standard threshold is 3+ trades; cite Xactimate best practices

## Effective Counter-Arguments
1. **Material misidentification** — photographs and close-ups of actual materials vs. what carrier scoped. This is the strongest argument because it's objectively verifiable.
2. **EagleView area discrepancy** — 96 SF vs. 2,388 SF is indefensible. Even a reasonable margin of error doesn't explain a 25x difference.
3. **RCNYS code requirements** — full code citation table for every omitted component forces the carrier to address code compliance
4. **HAAG criteria for comp shingles** — circular indentation + granule displacement + mat exposure = functional damage, not cosmetic
5. **5-trade O&P** — well above the 3-trade industry threshold

## Pricing Patterns
- Carrier used corrugated fiberglass pricing ($5.69/SF) for structures that require comp shingle ($337.78/SQ) and metal panel ($15.11/SF) pricing
- The pricing error follows from the material misidentification — if you call everything "fiberglass," you get fiberglass pricing
- **Key insight:** Correcting the material identification automatically corrects the pricing by an order of magnitude
