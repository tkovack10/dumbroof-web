Executive Summary</h2> <p>On October 18, 2025, a wind event impacted the property located at 5912 Illinois 148, Waltonville, IL. A forensic inspection was conducted and documented across 49 photographs, evaluating the Architectural Laminated Comp Shingle roof system (Tamko dimensional profile), the EPDM rubber membrane low-slope transition, associated flashings, gutter apron details, and the underlying 1×12 nominal plank decking and ventilation infrastructure.</p> <p>The inspection documented widespread wind damage across multiple roof slopes in a directional pattern consistent with the October 18, 2025 storm event. Chalk-marked test squares quantified significant wind-crease counts—17 wind-damaged shingles on the west slope and 15+ on the rear slope—along with broken factory seal strips and wind-displaced tabs exposing nail lines with no fastener oxidation, confirming recent-origin loss rather than long-term weathering. A large section of shingles on the south slope was wind-displaced, exposing the EPDM membrane underlayment and necessitating emergency tarping with staged GAF Ice & Water Shield. Step flashing at wall-to-roof transitions was found compromised, and the EPDM-to-shingle transition flashing showed wind-induced lifting that breached the minimum 18-inch membrane overlap detail.</p> <p>Full roof system replacement is warranted on both technical and code-compliance grounds. The Tamko dimensional shingle's approximately 40-inch length, 13-inch exposure, and 7-inch tab width on 1×12 plank decking make isolated spot repairs structurally infeasible, as new shingles cannot achieve proper seal-strip engagement with surrounding wind-fatigued courses whose factory bonds are already broken. Furthermore, the existing ventilation—limited to two box vents—falls below IRC Section R806.1 net free ventilation area requirements for the attic space, a code deficiency that must be corrected when the roof covering is replaced under the current IL-adopted IRC. The convergence of multi-slope wind damage, non-repairable seal-strip failures, compromised EPDM transition flashing, and mandatory ventilation upgrades establishes that piecemeal repair cannot restore the roof assembly to a code-compliant, weather-tight condition.</p> <!-- SECTION 3: STORM EVENT OVERVIEW --> <div style="margin-top:24pt;"></div> <h2>3.
