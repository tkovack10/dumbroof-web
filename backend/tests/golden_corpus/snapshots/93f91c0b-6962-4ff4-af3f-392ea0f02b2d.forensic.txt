Executive Summary</h2> <p>On July 03, 2025, the residential property at 74 Crestmont Rd, Binghamton, NY sustained storm damage from a hail event affecting its 3-Tab 25-year composition shingle roof system. A comprehensive forensic inspection was conducted, producing 50 photographs documenting the roof's condition across multiple slopes. The inspection employed systematic chalk-marked test squares and individual impact analysis to quantify hail strike density and assess the extent of functional damage to the existing shingle system.</p> <p>Chalk-marked test squares on both front and rear roof slopes confirmed widespread hail impacts with granule displacement and underlying mat bruising at marked strike locations. One documented test square recorded a density of approximately 30 confirmed hail hits (H=30), with consistent strike patterns observed across all tested slopes. Close-up photography verified circular impact signatures characteristic of hail-induced damage, including fractured granule bonds and exposed fiberglass mat, confirming that the damage mechanism is atmospheric impact rather than normal weathering or wear.</p> <p>Full roof system replacement is warranted on a technical basis. The existing 3-Tab 25-year shingles exhibit an aged profile with granule erosion and dimensional characteristics that cannot be matched by current metric shingle products, rendering spot repair infeasible under RCNYS material-matching and weatherproofing requirements. The confirmed hail damage density across multiple slopes exceeds any reasonable threshold for isolated repair, and the compromised granule-to-mat bond across the roof field has reduced the system's remaining service life and weather resistance below functional performance standards, necessitating full slope replacement to restore the roof to a code-compliant, weather-tight condition.</p> <!-- SECTION 3: STORM EVENT OVERVIEW --> <div style="margin-top:24pt;"></div> <h2>3.
