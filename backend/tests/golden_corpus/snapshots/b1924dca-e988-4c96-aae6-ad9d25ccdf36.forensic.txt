Executive Summary</h2> <p>On March 10, 2026, the property located at 4854 NW Homestead Terrace, Riverside, MO 64150, USA sustained damage from a combined hail and wind storm event. The roofing system consists of TAMKO architectural laminated composite shingles, confirmed via manufacturer markings on the back side of extracted field samples. A forensic inspection of the roof and associated components was conducted, with 10 photographs documenting damage conditions across all visible roof slopes.</p> <p>Field inspection confirmed widespread hail and wind damage distributed across the entire roof system. Chalk-circle testing of field shingles identified numerous hail impacts exhibiting severe granule displacement, exposed asphalt mat, and subsurface mat bruising — consistent with hail of sufficient velocity to cause functional damage. Macro close-up examination further revealed embedded granule fracture and mat cracking at impact sites. Wind damage was simultaneously documented, with test-square analysis indicating ten or more mechanically separated shingles on a single slope, corroborated by compromised seal-strip adhesion observed on extracted sample backs.</p> <p>Full roof system replacement is warranted based on the uniform distribution of both hail and wind damage across all inspected slopes, rendering selective spot repair technically infeasible. Isolated repairs cannot restore the compromised granule-to-mat bond or re-establish factory seal-strip adhesion on wind-lifted shingles, and replacement shingles cannot be color- or profile-matched to the weathered existing field due to manufacturing lot variation and in-service granule erosion.
