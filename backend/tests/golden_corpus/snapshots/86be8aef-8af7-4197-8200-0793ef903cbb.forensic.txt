Executive Summary</h2> <p>On May 22, 2024, the commercial property located at 139 Abbe Rd S, Elyria, OH 44035 sustained storm damage to its modified bitumen / flat roof system and associated building components. A forensic inspection was conducted to document the condition of the roofing membrane, flashings, edge metal, and ancillary systems in order to establish the cause, scope, and extent of storm-related damage. This report presents the findings of that investigation and provides a technical basis for the recommended scope of repair.</p> <p>The inspection identified storm-inflicted damage to the modified bitumen membrane consistent with impact forces and wind-driven stress. Observable indicators include puncturing, scuffing, and displacement of the membrane surface layer, along with deformation of soft-metal flashings and edge details that are characteristic of storm-event loading rather than long-term weathering. Gutter and drainage components exhibited mechanical deformation consistent with the same event. These damage signatures are distinguishable from normal wear patterns by their randomized distribution and acute fracture geometry.</p> <p>The modified bitumen roof system at this property cannot be restored to pre-loss condition through spot repair. Partial patching of a membrane system compromises the monolithic waterproofing envelope and does not meet manufacturer warranty requirements or the performance standards referenced under the RCO and IRC Section R905.11 for modified bitumen installations. The aged membrane cannot be material-matched in color, granule profile, or polymer composition, and selective replacement introduces differential expansion joints that become future failure points. Full system replacement is the only technically sound remediation that restores code-compliant performance.</p> <p>The carrier's approved scope of $6,019.87 does not adequately account for the full membrane replacement, code-required underlayment and flashing details, removal and disposal of the existing compromised system, or the edge-metal and drainage component repairs documented during the inspection. Our forensic analysis, grounded in the physical evidence at 139 Abbe Rd S, identifies a scope of necessary work that materially exceeds the carrier's current valuation.
