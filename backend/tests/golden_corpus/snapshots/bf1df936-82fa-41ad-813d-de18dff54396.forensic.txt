Executive Summary</h2> <p>On November 08, 2025, the property located at 5141 SC-72, Clinton, SC 29325, USA sustained storm damage from a hail event. A comprehensive forensic inspection documented conditions across 97 photographs, evaluating all exterior material systems including 3-Tab 25yr Comp Shingle roofing, vinyl siding and corner trim, aluminum gutters and downspouts, window assemblies and screens, and associated fascia and soffit components across all visible elevations.</p> <p>The inspection identified widespread hail impact damage distributed across multiple elevations and material systems. Vinyl siding panels exhibit confirmed circular hail impact punctures penetrating through to the substrate, compromising the weather envelope integrity of the structure. Aluminum downspouts display characteristic hail denting on flat-face surfaces, gutters show deformation consistent with hail strike force, and window screens document puncture and spider-web fracture patterns attributable to hail impact. Window glass on multiple units exhibits impact points, and window sills and frames show cracking consistent with hail-generated force loads.</p> <p>Full replacement of the damaged vinyl siding system is technically required due to the inability to color-match aged panels with new production material, a condition well-documented in manufacturer technical bulletins for UV-degraded vinyl substrates. Per the SCRC and underlying IRC standards, exterior cladding penetrations that breach the weather-resistive barrier must be remediated to restore code-compliant envelope performance — spot repairs over penetrated panels do not restore this compliance. The widespread distribution of hail impacts across all visible elevations, combined with the non-repairability of punctured vinyl panels and fractured window components, eliminates spot repair as a viable restoration methodology.</p> <p>The carrier's approved scope of $24,628.98 does not account for full siding replacement necessitated by panel penetration and color-match impossibility, complete gutter and downspout replacement across all affected elevations, window component restoration including damaged screens and cracked sill assemblies, and appropriate Overhead and Profit for the multi-trade coordination required across roofing, siding, gutter, and window trades.
