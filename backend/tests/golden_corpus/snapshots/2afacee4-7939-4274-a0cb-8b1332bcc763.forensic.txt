Conclusions &amp; Recommendations</h2> <p>Based on our forensic analysis of 48 documented findings, the property at 9600 Berkley Ln, St John, IN 46373, USA sustained confirmed storm damage from April 27, 2026. Rust-free exposed nails at blow-off zones confirm the damage is recent and consistent with the reported date of loss, while torn shingle mat at nail holes demonstrates forcible wind removal rather than age-related adhesive failure. Directional damage concentration on a single roof face, broken seal strips with a windward pattern, and displaced drip edge and fascia trim collectively establish a single directional wind event as the proximate cause of damage, with six documented IRC code violations confirming the property's weather envelope has been critically compromised.</p> <p>The confirmed wind damage to the architectural laminated composite shingle roof system and associated components — including drip edge, fascia trim, and soffit panels — requires full slope replacement rather than spot repair.
