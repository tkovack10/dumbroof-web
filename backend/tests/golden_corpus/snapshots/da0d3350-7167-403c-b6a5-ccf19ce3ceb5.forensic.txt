Executive Summary</h2> <p>On April 25, 2026, a hail event impacted the property located at 14221 Cedar Post Dr, Haslet, TX 76052, USA. This single-story, brick-veneer residence features architectural laminated composite shingles with an aluminum gutter and downspout drainage system. A forensic inspection comprising 54 photographs was conducted to document storm-related damage across the property's exterior envelope, with particular focus on soft-metal drainage components, fascia, soffit, and roofing transitions.</p> <p>Field inspection confirmed hail impact damage across multiple elevations of the gutter and downspout system. Chalk testing on aluminum downspouts revealed numerous circular dents on flat faces, a diagnostic pattern consistent with direct hail strike confirmed at multiple points along each run. Gutter troughs exhibit denting and oxidative splatter staining characteristic of hail exposure, while downspout seams show splitting from impact force. Fascia and soffit transitions display moisture intrusion indicators at critical junctures, and the lower roof section at the wall-to-roof junction presents compromised flashing conditions near the electric meter penetration.</p> <p>The documented damage to the aluminum gutter and downspout system necessitates full system replacement rather than spot repair. Dented aluminum cannot be restored to original profile or factory finish, and partial replacement of an aged, oxidized system creates galvanic incompatibility and visual discontinuity that fails material-matching standards under IRC and manufacturer installation specifications. Furthermore, the split downspout seams compromise the sealed integrity of the drainage path, and per Texas-adopted IRC Section R801.3 and related drainage provisions, the system must function as a continuous, code-compliant assembly — a standard that piecemeal repair of storm-compromised components cannot achieve.</p> <p>The carrier's recognized scope of $18,704.28 does not adequately account for full gutter and downspout system replacement across all affected elevations, necessary fascia and soffit remediation at moisture-compromised transitions, flashing repair at the documented wall-to-roof junction, or proper downspout extension to grade to prevent foundation water intrusion.
