Executive Summary</h2> <p>On March 31, 2026, the property at 7 scott ave , greene , NY sustained damage from a combined hail and wind event. A forensic inspection of the property documented conditions across 29 photographs, evaluating the main dwelling's architectural laminated composite shingle roof system, split-configuration siding (first-floor vinyl lap siding and second-floor cedar shingle siding), aluminum coil-stock window and door trim, downspouts, a storm door, an enclosed porch/sunroom, and a detached garage with aged asphalt shingle roofing. The inspection assessed all exterior material systems for both storm-induced damage and pre-existing conditions to establish a defensible causation baseline.</p> <p>Chalk testing on aluminum window trim, door trim, corner post trim, and downspouts confirmed multiple circular hail impact dents across several elevations, with exposed bare metal exhibiting rust-colored oxidation streaking consistent with recent storm origin. The storm door's raised-panel section displays hail impact denting with corresponding rust staining at exposed substrate. Wind damage is evidenced by warped and buckled vinyl siding panels consistent with positive and negative wind pressure displacement, a punctured porch screen consistent with wind-driven debris impact, and accelerated moisture infiltration through the already-deteriorated upper-story cedar shingle siding. The detached garage roof exhibits heavy moss colonization confirming long-term moisture retention that, combined with storm impacts, has rendered the shingle system non-functional.</p> <p>Full replacement of affected trades — siding, aluminum trim, storm door, and garage roofing — is warranted on both technical and code-compliance grounds. The upper-story cedar shingle siding exhibits severe paint failure and missing or broken units that preclude material-matched spot repair; per RCNYS standards referencing IRC weather-resistive barrier requirements, piecemeal patching of a compromised wall cladding system does not restore code-compliant performance. The mixed-material siding configuration, discontinuation of original cedar shingle profiles, and oxidation differences in aluminum coil stock make aesthetic and functional matching of isolated repairs infeasible.
