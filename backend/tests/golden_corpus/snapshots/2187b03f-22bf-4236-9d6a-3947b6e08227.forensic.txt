Executive Summary</h2> <p>On April 25, 2026, the property located at 614 E 26th St, IN, sustained storm damage affecting its modified bitumen / flat roof membrane system and associated building envelope components. A forensic inspection was conducted to document the condition of the roofing assembly, flashing details, drainage infrastructure, and ancillary building elements in relation to the reported storm event. The roof system consists of a modified bitumen membrane application, a material particularly susceptible to impact damage and wind-driven compromise at seams, laps, and perimeter terminations.</p> <p>Field examination of the modified bitumen membrane revealed impact-related damage consistent with storm activity, including puncture points, granule displacement, and compromised lap seams across the roof field. Perimeter flashing and edge metal details exhibited deformation and lifting consistent with wind loading forces generated during the April 25, 2026 event. These findings indicate that the storm event produced sufficient force to breach the weatherproofing integrity of the flat roof assembly, creating active or incipient moisture intrusion pathways that compromise the substrate and interior building envelope.</p> <p>The documented damage to the modified bitumen roof system necessitates full replacement rather than spot repair due to the inherent limitations of partial membrane restoration on an aged assembly. Sectional patching of a modified bitumen system cannot restore uniform adhesion, consistent granule coverage, or watertight lap integrity to meet the performance standards referenced under the IRC, and localized repairs to a weathered membrane introduce differential expansion zones that accelerate future failure.
