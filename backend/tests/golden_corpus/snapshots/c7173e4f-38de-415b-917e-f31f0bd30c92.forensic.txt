Executive Summary</h2> <p>On April 26, 2026, a hail-producing storm system impacted the property located at 500 S Main St, El Dorado Springs, MO 64744, USA. A comprehensive forensic inspection was conducted and documented across 97 photographs, encompassing two roofing systems present on the property: standing seam/R-panel metal roofing on the main structure and architectural laminated composition shingles on the lower structure. The inspection also evaluated associated components including ridge caps, roof penetration flashings, pipe boots, attic vents, and a rooftop HVAC curb assembly.</p> <p>Chalk testing performed across the metal roof panels confirmed widespread hail impact damage, with circular gaps in chalk lines documenting dent locations throughout the flat pan areas of multiple panels. Impact points exhibit coating disruption, indentation of the metal substrate, and oxidation splatter patterns consistent with hail strike signatures from the reported storm event. On the shingled roof section, inspector-marked hail impacts were documented adjacent to penetrations, and functional damage was observed where shingle mat exposure and granule displacement were present at butt edges — damage patterns characteristic of hail-induced fracture on architectural laminated shingles.</p> <p>The pervasive distribution of confirmed hail impacts across the metal roof system has compromised the protective galvalume coating at numerous points, rendering spot repair insufficient to restore weathertight integrity or arrest progressive corrosion at each impact site. On the shingled section, the combination of widespread hail-induced granule loss and mat fracturing precludes selective shingle replacement due to material discontinuity, color-lot unavailability, and the inability to achieve a code-compliant weather-resistant barrier through patchwork repairs under IRC Section R905.2.
