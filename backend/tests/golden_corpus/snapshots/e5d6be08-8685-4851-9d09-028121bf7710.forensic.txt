Executive Summary</h2> <p>On September 26, 2025, the property at 4833 W Christine Cir, Glendale, AZ 85308, USA sustained damage from a hail-producing storm system. The residence is a two-story dwelling with architectural laminated composition shingle roofing, stucco and brick exterior cladding, and multiple roof-mounted systems including solar panel arrays, package HVAC units, and turbine ventilation. A comprehensive forensic inspection documented 93 photographs across all material systems, including roofing surfaces, soft metals, interior ceilings, exterior trim, and rooftop mechanical equipment.</p> <p>Hail impact damage was confirmed across multiple material systems through chalk-circle testing and tape-measure verification on the roof surface. Architectural laminated shingles exhibit granule displacement and exposed mat fibers consistent with direct hail strikes, while rooftop soft metals — including turbine vents and HVAC unit casings — display characteristic circular deformation patterns confirming hail of sufficient size and kinetic energy to damage the roof system. Interior inspection of multiple rooms revealed active water staining and saturation on textured ceilings, confirming that the roof membrane has been breached and is permitting moisture intrusion into the building envelope. Exterior fascia and soffit trim at the garage elevation show cracking and separation at the soffit-to-fascia junction, further evidencing storm-related damage to ancillary roof-edge components.</p> <p>Full roof system replacement is warranted on the basis of non-repairability. The existing shingle exposure measures approximately 5.2 inches, a legacy pre-metric dimension that precludes color, profile, and dimensional matching with any currently manufactured replacement product, rendering spot repair non-compliant with AZ-IRC standards requiring uniform material performance across the roof plane.
