2: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY --> <div style="margin-top:24pt;"></div> <h2>2. Executive Summary</h2> <p>On March 05, 2024, a combined hail and wind storm event impacted the property located at 6038 Pin Oak Ct, Royse City, TX 75189, USA, in Rockwall County, Texas. A comprehensive forensic inspection was conducted, producing 48 photographs documenting storm-related damage to the architectural laminated composite shingle roof system on the main dwelling, as well as a metal shop roof with spray foam insulation and an associated metal shed on the same property. This report establishes the causal relationship between the reported storm event and the observed damage across all roofing systems on the property.</p> <p>Systematic chalk-circle testing across multiple roof slopes confirmed widespread hail impacts exhibiting circular depressions, granule displacement exposing the underlying asphalt mat, and confirmed mat bruising consistent with functional damage per HAAG Engineering standards. A documented test square on the front/right slope recorded ten or more hail strikes within a standard 10×10-foot area, exceeding accepted insurance replacement thresholds. Wind damage was simultaneously confirmed through broken seal strips, directional shingle lift patterns, and sharp wind-crease fold lines observed near nail lines on upper courses — a distribution pattern consistent with combined hail and wind exposure rather than isolated wear or aging.</p> <p>Full replacement of the composite shingle roof system is technically required. The density and uniform distribution of confirmed hail impacts and wind damage across all inspected slopes preclude spot repair as a viable remediation strategy, as selective shingle replacement cannot restore the roof's compromised weather-tight integrity and will result in material-matching deficiencies in color, profile, and granule composition. Furthermore, any partial tear-off and re-roofing activity triggers compliance with the current Texas-adopted IRC (International Residential Code) provisions for underlayment, fastening schedules, and ventilation — code-upgrade requirements that apply to the full roof deck once repair scope exceeds localized patching thresholds.</p> <p>The carrier's recognized scope at $53,194.95 does not appear to account for the metal shop roof with spray foam insulation, the metal shed structure, required overhead and profit for the multi-trade coordination involved, or the full extent of code-compliance upgrades triggered by roof system replacement under the Texas-adopted IRC. Our forensic analysis, grounded in 48 field photographs, 50 documented key findings, and standardized hail-testing methodology, identifies a total scope of necessary repairs that extends materially beyond the carrier's currently approved amount.
