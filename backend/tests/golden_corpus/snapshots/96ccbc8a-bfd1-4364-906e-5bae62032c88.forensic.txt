Executive Summary</h2> <p>On April 14, 2026, the property at 440 Christensen Rd, Waterloo, IA 50701, USA sustained damage from a hail-producing storm system. A forensic inspection was conducted and documented across 20 photographs, evaluating the architectural laminated composite shingle roof system on the primary dwelling, the flat EPDM/rolled membrane roof section over the attached garage, associated penetration flashings, soft-metal gutters and trim, and exterior siding components. This report establishes causation and documents the scope of hail damage to the property's roofing and exterior systems.</p> <p>The inspection confirmed pervasive hail damage across all visible roof slopes and the garage membrane. Architectural laminated shingles exhibit widespread circular hail impacts with granule displacement exposing the dark asphalt mat beneath — functional damage confirmed per HAAG Engineering standards. The flat EPDM membrane section displays dozens of circular hail impact depressions consistent with the same storm event. Soft-metal components — including aluminum gutters, drip edge, and trim — show deformation consistent with hail strike patterns, while penetration flashings at the chimney B-vent collar and pipe boots exhibit catastrophic hail fracture with cracked sealant joints confirmed as impact-induced rather than age-related deterioration.</p> <p>The uniform distribution of hail impacts across all roof slopes, penetration flashings, and the EPDM membrane renders spot repair technically infeasible; selective shingle replacement cannot restore system integrity when damage density is pervasive and replacement materials cannot match the weathered profile, granule color, or dimensional characteristics of the existing aged shingles. Additionally, any re-roofing activity on this structure triggers compliance with the IRC as adopted in Iowa, including current underlayment, flashing, and fastening requirements, necessitating a full system approach.
