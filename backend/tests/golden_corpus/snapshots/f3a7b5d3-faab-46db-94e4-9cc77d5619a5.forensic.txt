Executive Summary</h2> <p>On July 03, 2025, a hail event impacted the property at 49 Crestmont Rd, Binghamton, NY 13905, USA, a Tudor-style residence featuring a natural slate roof system, stucco exterior with decorative half-timber elements, wood trim assemblies, fiber cement and wood lap siding on secondary elevations, and soft-metal gutter and downspout components. A forensic field inspection documented conditions across 62 photographs, encompassing the main dwelling's steep-pitch multi-gable roof with complex valley geometry, an attached dormer structure, an enclosed porch, and a detached garage with a low-slope roof. The material systems at issue span roofing, exterior cladding, glazing, window and fascia trim, and sheet-metal drainage components.</p> <p>Chalk testing on downspout corners and gutter surfaces confirmed repeatable circular hail-impact dents on soft-metal components across multiple elevations, establishing the presence and caliber of hail strike evidence at the property. The natural slate roof exhibits displaced and fractured tile units at the mid-course transition between floors, with individual slates showing brittle fracture patterns consistent with direct hail impact on an aged, inelastic substrate. Fiber cement and wood lap siding sections display chalk-verified circular dent patterns on multiple elevations, while window trim areas reveal exposed sheathing and rust staining indicative of storm-driven moisture intrusion at compromised envelope penetrations. The dormer transition zone shows missing shingles and exposed underlayment, and gutter troughs contain accumulated granule debris consistent with roof surface degradation accelerated by hail impact.</p> <p>Full replacement of the natural slate roof system is required because the existing aged slate has become brittle and inelastic, rendering individual unit replacement technically infeasible — replacement slate cannot be dimensionally or structurally matched to the remaining field, and the installation of new units into a compromised surrounding matrix does not restore watertight integrity as required under RCNYS roof-covering provisions for weather protection. The same material-matching impossibility applies to the affected wood and fiber cement siding courses, where hail impacts have breached the painted substrate envelope and spot repairs cannot replicate the existing profile, exposure, or protective coating system. These conditions, documented through chalk-test confirmation, fracture-pattern analysis, and moisture-intrusion evidence, establish that the July 03, 2025 hail event functionally defeated multiple exterior envelope systems that cannot be restored to pre-loss condition through localized repair.</p> <!-- SECTION 3: STORM EVENT OVERVIEW --> <div style="margin-top:24pt;"></div> <h2>3.
